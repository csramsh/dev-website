The CSRA Mesh community started after Hurricane Helene made clear just how fragile our communication infrastructure really is. When the power went out and cell towers went down across Augusta, Evans, and the surrounding area, information became scarce fast.

Meshtastic offers something different: a mesh network of small, inexpensive LoRa radios that talk directly to each other — no internet, no cell service, no third-party infrastructure required. Every node that joins the network makes the whole thing stronger and reaches further.

## What We're Building

We're working to build a connected mesh across the CSRA that covers Augusta, Evans, Grovetown, North Augusta, Aiken, Clearwater, and beyond. Nodes are being placed on rooftops, solar-powered and running 24/7, creating a backbone that the whole community can use.

## Who's Involved

Our community includes Amateur Radio operators, GMRS operators, makers, preppers, emergency communicators, and plain old tech enthusiasts. You don't need a license to use Meshtastic — just curiosity and a node.

## How to Get Involved

The easiest way is to join us on Discord or Facebook. If you're ready to jump in, head over to the [FAQs & Guides](/faqs) page and follow the Quick Start Guide to get your first node on the mesh.
